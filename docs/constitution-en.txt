Association Articles — Konkani Kutam e.V.

§1 Name, Registered Office, Fiscal Year, and Operational Center

1. The association shall bear the name "Konkani Kutam e.V."
2. Its official registered seat is at Klosterstraße 6, 51709 Marienheide, and it shall be entered in the register of associations.
3. The operational center of the association is in Frankfurt am Main, where the main activities and events of the association will take place. The association may also conduct activities nationwide.
4. The fiscal year is the calendar year.

§2 Purpose of the Association

1. The association exclusively and directly pursues charitable purposes within the meaning of the section "tax-privileged purposes" of the German Fiscal Code (Abgabenordnung – AO).
2. The purpose of the association is the promotion of: Konkani Language; Konkani Catholic culture and traditions; Konkani education and upbringing; intercultural understanding and promoting integration into German society.
3. The purpose of the statutes is realized in particular through: organization of cultural events, language and cooking classes; promotion of exchange programs between cultures; support of children and youth in educational and social projects; cooperation with other non-profit organizations.

§3 Non-Profit Status

1. The association is selflessly active; it does not primarily pursue its own economic purposes.
2. Funds of the association may only be used for purposes in accordance with the statutes.
3. Members do not receive payments from the association's funds.
4. No person may benefit from expenses that are unrelated to the purposes of the association or from disproportionately high remunerations.

§4 Membership

1. Any natural or legal person who supports the goals of the association may become a member.
2. Types of membership:
a. Konkani Catholics residing in Germany.
b. Spouses and family members of Konkani Catholics.
c. Individuals with genuine interest in Konkani culture and community (associate membership).
3. Membership is obtained by written application, which is decided upon by the executive board.
4. Membership must be renewed each year before March.
5. Membership ends through resignation, expulsion, or death. Resignation must be declared in writing to the board.

§5 Rights and Duties of Members

1. Only registered members are allowed to vote.
2. Members have the right to attend general assembly meetings.
3. Members are obliged to promote the goals of the association and to pay contributions as decided by the general assembly.

§6 Organs of the Association

The organs of the association are:
1. the General Assembly,
2. the Executive Board.

§7 General Assembly

1. The general assembly shall be held at least once a year.
2. It is in particular responsible for: election and dismissal of the executive board; acceptance of the annual report; discharge of the executive board; determination of membership contributions; resolutions on amendments to the statutes; resolutions on the dissolution of the association.
3. An extraordinary general assembly shall be convened if required in the interest of the association or upon written request by one third of the members.

§8 Executive Board

1. The executive board consists of 4 to 8 members, including: the Chairperson, the Deputy Chairperson, the Treasurer, the Secretary, and up to four additional board members.
2. The board is elected by the general assembly for a term of three years.
3. The association shall be represented in and out of court by two members of the executive board, one of whom must be the Chairperson or Deputy Chairperson.

§9 Resolutions

1. Resolutions of the general assembly are passed by a simple majority of the votes cast.
2. Amendments to the statutes require a two-thirds majority of the members present.

§10 Auditors

1. The general assembly shall elect two auditors for a term of two years.
2. Auditors may not be members of the board.
3. The auditors shall examine the orderly bookkeeping and report to the general assembly.

§11 Dissolution of the Association

1. The dissolution of the association may only be decided at a general assembly with a three-fourths majority of the members present.
2. In the event of dissolution or termination of the association, the assets of the association shall pass to the city of Frankfurt, which shall use them exclusively and directly for charitable purposes in the field of culture and education.
